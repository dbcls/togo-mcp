What is TogoMCP?

TogoMCP is a comprehensive Model Context Protocol (MCP) server that provides LLM agents with access to a vast ecosystem of life sciences databases through SPARQL queries, RDF data exploration, and ID conversion services. Developed by the Database Center for Life Science (DBCLS) in Japan, it integrates over 20 major biological and biomedical databases.

The server offers researchers a powerful toolkit for:

  • Cross-database queries: Seamlessly query multiple databases with a single interface
  • Data integration: Combine information from different sources using semantic web technologies
  • Knowledge discovery: Uncover connections between proteins, diseases, drugs, and more
  • ID conversion: Convert between different database identifiers effortlessly

Whether you're a biologist exploring protein functions, a clinician researching disease mechanisms, or a pharmaceutical researcher investigating drug targets, TogoMCP empowers your AI assistant to access and integrate the data you need.

Usage Examples

Here are some real-world examples demonstrating TogoMCP's capabilities:

Example 1: Multi-Database Protein-Disease-Drug Analysis

Prompt: Find proteins that are associated with both cardiovascular diseases and have known small molecule inhibitors in ChEMBL, and classify them according to disease and drug availability.

Response:

The assistant conducted a comprehensive multi-database integration by first identifying cardiovascular disease-related proteins through MeSH, then querying ChEMBL for protein targets with known inhibitors. It systematically analyzed 75+ human proteins associated with cardiovascular diseases, categorizing them by disease type (hypertension, coronary artery disease, heart failure, arrhythmias) and drug development status (approved drugs, clinical trials, failed programs).

Tools Used:
  • TogoMCP:list_databases - Surveyed available databases
  • TogoMCP:search_mesh_entity - Identified cardiovascular disease terms
  • TogoMCP:search_chembl_target - Found drug targets
  • TogoMCP:get_MIE_file - Retrieved ChEMBL schema and examples
  • TogoMCP:run_sparql - Executed multiple SPARQL queries across ChEMBL database
Key Results:
  • Identified 75+ proteins with cardiovascular disease associations and small molecule inhibitors
  • Classified targets by disease category: Hypertension (15+), Coronary Artery Disease (20+), Heart Failure (12+), etc.
  • Most productive target families: Sodium Channels (57 inhibitors), L-Type Calcium Channels (27), Beta-1 Adrenergic Receptor (25+)
  • Drug development breakdown: 50+ targets with approved drugs, 15+ in Phase 3 trials

Example 2: Enzyme Classification Distribution Analysis

Prompt: Determine the distribution of human enzymes by their EC number classes.

Response:

The assistant queried UniProt's Swiss-Prot database to retrieve all human enzymes with EC numbers, then aggregated them by EC class. The analysis revealed the distribution across all seven enzyme classes, showing that transferases and hydrolases together account for 78.3% of all human enzymes, reflecting the importance of cellular signaling and regulatory processes.

Tools Used:
  • TogoMCP:get_MIE_file - Retrieved UniProt schema and SPARQL examples
  • TogoMCP:run_sparql - Executed queries to extract and aggregate enzyme data by EC class
Key Results:
  • Analyzed 4,442 unique human enzymes from Swiss-Prot
  • EC 2 - Transferases: 1,823 proteins (41.0%) - Most abundant
  • EC 3 - Hydrolases: 1,655 proteins (37.3%) - Second most abundant
  • EC 1 - Oxidoreductases: 545 proteins (12.3%)
  • Smaller classes (EC 4-7) represent specialized metabolic functions (2.3-3.4% each)

Example 3: Natural Products vs Synthetic Compounds Comparative Analysis

Prompt: Use ChEBI (chemical classification) + ChEMBL (bioactivity) + PubChem (chemical descriptors) to compare the bioactivity profiles and chemical diversity of natural products versus synthetic compounds across different therapeutic areas.

Response:

The assistant conducted an integrated analysis across three chemical databases, comparing natural products (e.g., vinblastine, paclitaxel) with synthetic drugs (e.g., imatinib, omeprazole). The analysis revealed distinct patterns: natural products showed ultra-high potency (70% with IC50 < 100 nM) and polypharmacology (8.5 targets on average), while synthetic compounds demonstrated selective potency and monopharmacology (2-3 targets), with natural products dominating oncology (~60% of anticancer drugs) and synthetic drugs excelling in cardiovascular and CNS diseases.

Tools Used:
  • TogoMCP:get_MIE_file - Retrieved schemas for ChEBI, ChEMBL, and PubChem
  • TogoMCP:run_sparql - Executed queries across multiple databases
  • TogoMCP:get_pubchem_compound_id - Retrieved PubChem identifiers
  • TogoMCP:get_compound_attributes_from_pubchem - Obtained molecular descriptors
Key Results:
  • Natural products: 70% show IC50 < 100 nM, average 8 chiral centers, MW 400-900 Da, hit 8.5 targets on average
  • Synthetic compounds: 40% show IC50 < 100 nM, average 1 chiral center, MW 300-600 Da, hit 2-3 targets
  • Natural products dominant in oncology (~60% of anticancer drugs)
  • Synthetic drugs excel in cardiovascular, CNS, and metabolic diseases
  • Complementary strengths suggest optimal strategy combines both approaches

Setup Guide

TogoMCP can be integrated with various AI platforms. Choose the setup method appropriate for your platform:

Claude Desktop

Method 1: Custom Connectors (Recommended for Paid Plans)

  1. Open Claude Desktop and navigate to Settings → Connectors
  2. Click "Add custom connector" at the bottom of the Connectors section
  3. Enter the TogoMCP server URL: 
    https://togomcp.rdfportal.org/mcp
  4. Click "Add" to complete the configuration
  5. The connector will now appear in your available tools

Method 2: JSON Configuration (Alternative)

  1. Navigate to Settings → Developer → Edit Config
  2. Locate your claude_desktop_config.json file:
    • macOS: ~/Library/Application Support/Claude/claude_desktop_config.json
    • Windows: %APPDATA%\Claude\claude_desktop_config.json
  3. Add the TogoMCP server configuration (note: remote servers should be added via Connectors UI, not JSON config)

ChatGPT

Available for: Business, Enterprise, and Education plans only

For Workspace Admins:

  1. Navigate to Workspace Settings → Permissions & Roles
  2. Enable "Connected Data Developer mode / Create custom MCP connectors"
  3. Go to Settings → Connectors → Create
  4. Fill in the connector details:
    • Name: TogoMCP
    • Description: Access to life sciences databases via RDF Portal
    • Connector URL: https://togomcp.rdfportal.org/mcp
  5. Click "Create" to add the connector
  6. In a new chat, click the "+" button near the message composer
  7. Select "More" and choose "TogoMCP" from the available connectors

Gemini CLI

Important: TogoMCP uses Streamable-HTTP transport, not SSE.

Configuration:

  1. Locate your Gemini CLI settings file:
    • User config: ~/.gemini/settings.json
    • Project config: .gemini/settings.json
  2. Add the TogoMCP server to the mcpServers section: 
    {
  "mcpServers": {
    "togomcp": {
      "httpUrl": "https://togomcp.rdfportal.org/mcp"
    }
  }
}
  3. Alternatively, use the CLI command: 
    gemini mcp add --transport http togomcp https://togomcp.rdfportal.org/mcp
  4. Verify the connection by running /mcp in Gemini CLI
  5. You should see TogoMCP listed among available servers

Available Databases

TogoMCP provides access to over 20 curated life sciences databases covering proteins, chemicals, diseases, genomics, and more:

UniProt

Comprehensive protein sequence and functional information. Contains 444M proteins including Swiss-Prot (manually curated) and TrEMBL (automatically annotated) with sequences, functions, domains, structures, and variants.

PubChem

Public database of chemical molecules and biological activities. Contains 119M compounds with molecular descriptors, 339M substances, 1.7M bioassays, and links to genes, proteins, pathways, and diseases.

ChEMBL

Manually curated database of bioactive molecules with drug-like properties. Contains 2.4M+ compounds, 1.6M assays, 20M bioactivity measurements for drug discovery research.

PDB

3D structural data for proteins, nucleic acids, and complexes from X-ray, NMR, and cryo-EM. Contains 204K+ entries with experimental methods, resolution, and cross-references.

ChEBI

Ontology database containing 217K+ chemical entities of biological interest including small molecules, atoms, ions, with hierarchical classification and biological roles.

Gene Ontology (GO)

Controlled vocabulary of terms for gene and gene product attributes across all organisms. Contains 48K+ terms in biological_process, molecular_function, and cellular_component domains.

MONDO

Comprehensive disease ontology integrating multiple disease databases into unified classification. Provides cross-references to OMIM, Orphanet, DOID, MESH, ICD, and 35+ databases.

MeSH

National Library of Medicine's controlled vocabulary thesaurus for biomedical literature indexing. Hierarchical structure with descriptors, qualifiers, and supplementary records.

Reactome

Open-source, curated knowledgebase of biological pathways and processes. Contains 22K+ pathways across 30+ species with molecular interactions and disease associations.

Rhea

Expert-curated database of biochemical reactions. Contains 17K reactions (34K directional representations), all atom-balanced and chemically annotated with ChEBI compounds.

NCBI Taxonomy

Comprehensive biological taxonomic classification covering 3M+ organisms from bacteria to mammals with hierarchical relationships and scientific/common names.

NCBI Gene

Gene database with 57M+ entries covering protein-coding genes, ncRNAs, pseudogenes across all organisms with gene symbols, descriptions, and chromosomal locations.

Ensembl

Comprehensive genomics database providing genome annotations for 100+ species with genes, transcripts, proteins, and exons with genomic locations.

ClinVar

Aggregates genomic variation and its relationship to human health with 3.5M+ variant records, clinical interpretations, and disease associations.

MedGen

NCBI's portal for medical conditions with a genetic component. Contains 233K+ clinical concepts integrating OMIM, Orphanet, HPO, and MONDO data.

PubMed

Bibliographic information for biomedical literature from MEDLINE. Includes publication metadata, abstracts, authors, and MeSH term annotations.

PubTator

Biomedical entity annotations extracted from PubMed literature using text mining. Contains disease and gene annotations linked to articles.

DDBJ

DNA Data Bank of Japan provides nucleotide sequence data from INSDC with genomic annotations, taxonomic classification, and functional annotations.

GlyCosmos

Comprehensive glycoscience portal integrating glycan structures (GlyTouCan), glycoproteins, glycosylation sites, and lectin-glycan interactions.

BacDive

Bacterial Diversity Metadatabase with 97K+ strain records containing standardized information on taxonomy, morphology, physiology, and cultivation conditions.

MediaDive

Microbial culture media database from DSMZ with 3,289 standardized recipes for bacteria, archaea, fungi, and microalgae.

NANDO

Comprehensive ontology for Japanese intractable (rare) diseases with 2,777 disease classes, multilingual labels, and cross-references to MONDO.

Available Tools

TogoMCP provides a comprehensive set of 35 tools for querying and analyzing life sciences data:

General & SPARQL Tools

TogoMCP_Usage_Guide

Get comprehensive usage guidelines and best practices for using TogoMCP tools effectively. Essential starting point for new users.

list_databases

List all available databases with their descriptions and summaries. Useful for discovering what data sources are accessible.

get_MIE_file

Retrieve the MIE (Model, Instance, Example) file for a specific database. Contains ShEx schema, RDF examples, and sample SPARQL queries.

get_sparql_example

Get example SPARQL queries for a specific database. Helpful for understanding query structure and best practices.

run_sparql

Execute a SPARQL query on a specific RDF database. The core tool for retrieving data from any integrated database.

get_sparql_endpoints

Get the list of available SPARQL endpoints for all databases. Returns database names and their corresponding endpoint URLs.

get_graph_list

Get a list of named graphs in a specific RDF database. Useful for understanding data organization within databases.

keyword_search_instructions

Get specific instructions for keyword searching in a particular database. Provides guidance on search syntax and best practices.

Database Search Tools

search_uniprot_entity

Search for UniProt protein entities by query string. Searches protein names, descriptions, AND disease associations. Returns protein IDs and basic information.

search_pdb_entity

Search PDBj for structural entries by keywords. Supports searches in PDB (protein structures), CC (chemical components), and PRD (biological molecules) databases.

search_chembl_molecule

Search for ChEMBL molecules by query string. Returns compound information and identifiers for bioactive molecules.

search_chembl_target

Search for ChEMBL protein targets by query string. Useful for finding drug target information and bioactivity data.

search_chembl_id_lookup

Lookup ChEMBL entities by ID or name. Provides quick access to ChEMBL compound and target information.

search_mesh_entity

Search for MeSH terms and concepts. Returns MeSH IDs and descriptors for medical subject headings.

search_reactome_entity

Search the Reactome knowledgebase for pathways, reactions, and complexes. Supports filtering by species and entity types.

search_rhea_entity

Search Rhea database for biochemical reactions using keyword search. Returns reaction IDs and equations for matching reactions.

PubChem Tools

get_pubchem_compound_id

Get PubChem compound IDs from compound names. Essential for retrieving chemical information from PubChem.

get_compound_attributes_from_pubchem

Retrieve comprehensive compound attributes from PubChem RDF including molecular properties, descriptors, and cross-references.

TogoID Conversion Tools

togoid_convertId

Convert IDs from one database to another using TogoID service. Supports conversion between 30+ biological databases with flexible routing.

togoid_countId

Count how many IDs can be converted between databases. Useful for understanding data coverage before conversion.

togoid_getAllDataset

Get configuration for all available datasets in TogoID including labels, regex patterns, prefixes, and examples.

togoid_getDataset

Get configuration for a specific dataset including validation patterns, URI prefixes, and example IDs.

togoid_getAllRelation

Get all possible conversion relationships between databases. Shows which database pairs can be converted.

togoid_getRelation

Get relationship details between two specific databases including forward, reverse, and bidirectional conversion support.

togoid_getDescription

Get descriptions for all databases in English and Japanese with organization information.

NCBI E-utilities Search Tools

ncbi_search_pubmed_entity

Search PubMed via NCBI E-utilities for biomedical literature. Supports PubMed/Entrez query syntax and returns PMIDs.

ncbi_search_ncbigene_entity

Search NCBI Gene database for genes matching keywords. Supports organism filtering and Entrez syntax. Returns gene IDs.

ncbi_search_taxonomy_entity

Search NCBI Taxonomy database for organisms. Returns taxonomy IDs for matching organism names or classifications.

ncbi_search_pubchem_compound_entity

Search PubChem Compound database for unique chemical structures. Returns PubChem Compound IDs (CIDs).

ncbi_search_pubchem_substance_entity

Search PubChem Substance database for depositor-provided chemical records. Returns PubChem Substance IDs (SIDs).

ncbi_search_pubchem_assay_entity

Search PubChem BioAssay database for biological screening data. Returns PubChem Assay IDs (AIDs).

ncbi_search_clinvar_entity

Search ClinVar for genetic variants and clinical interpretations. Returns ClinVar variation IDs.

ncbi_search_medgen_entity

Search MedGen for medical genetics concepts and conditions. Returns MedGen concept IDs.

NCBI E-utilities Data Retrieval Tools

ncbi_esummary

Fetch summary information for given NCBI database IDs using esummary. Returns detailed metadata after esearch.

ncbi_efetch

Fetch full records using efetch. Returns actual data (sequences, records, etc.) in requested format (XML, FASTA, GenBank, etc.).

Key Features: Ontology search, term lookup, hierarchy navigation, cross-references

Related Resources

RDF Portal

The RDF Portal is a comprehensive repository for semantic data in life sciences, hosted by the National Bioscience Database Center (NBDC) in Japan. It serves as a central hub for RDF datasets developed by Japanese research groups, with all datasets reviewed by NBDC for quality, interoperability, and queryability. The portal uses Semantic Web technologies to enhance data integration across diverse biological databases.

Features: SPARQL endpoints for 20+ databases, quality-reviewed RDF datasets, schema diagrams, example queries, and integration with international databases like UniProt, PDB, and PubChem.

TogoID

TogoID is an ID conversion service that enables seamless conversion between different biological database identifiers. It supports over 30 databases covering genes, proteins, compounds, diseases, and more. TogoID makes it easy to navigate between different data sources and integrate information across multiple databases.

Features: Convert IDs between 30+ databases, flexible routing for multi-step conversions, batch conversion support, RESTful API access, and integration with RDF Portal databases.

DBCLS (Database Center for Life Science)

The Database Center for Life Science (DBCLS) is a Japanese research center dedicated to promoting the development and integration of life science databases. DBCLS develops core technologies for database integration based on RDF and the Semantic Web, and organizes the annual BioHackathon series to encourage discussions about applying semantic web technologies to life science databases.

Projects: RDF Portal, TogoID, TogoMCP, TogoTV (educational videos), SPARQLthon (monthly hackathons), and various other tools for database integration and semantic web applications in life sciences.
